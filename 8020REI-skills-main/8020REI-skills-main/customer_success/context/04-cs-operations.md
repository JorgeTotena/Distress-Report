## 1. About 8020REI

### What We Do

**8020REI** (80/20 Real Estate Investors) is a data-as-a-service company serving professional real estate investors across the United States. The name comes from the Pareto principle: focus on the 20% of actions that drive 80% of results.

We provide investors with exclusive, data-driven market intelligence to find deals before their competition does.

**The formula:** 50% Strategy + 30% Great Data + 20% Tactics = Results.

We deliver all three. Most competitors only provide raw data (the 30%) and leave strategy and tactics to the investor.

### Business Model

- **Subscription-based SaaS** with county-level data protection
- Clients pay ~$2,300-$2,500/month on average (varies by tier and county count) for access to protected counties
- Each county has a **limited number of seats** — a few members share the data, but seats are capped
- This is NOT single-client exclusivity. Multiple investors share a county, but far fewer than mass-market providers
- **~140 active clients** across **1,200+ protected counties** nationwide
- Revenue = Monthly subscription (MRR) + one-time Onboarding Fee
- **Deal Value formula:** (MRR x 12) + Onboarding Fee

### The Feedback Loop — Our Core Competitive Flywheel

This is what makes 8020REI fundamentally different from data-only providers:

```
1. SHARE — Client reports monthly results (closed deals, bad contacts, opt-outs)
     ↓
2. OPTIMIZE — AI models learn from outcomes, refining targeting criteria
     ↓
3. TARGET — Next month's data lists are sharper, with higher-probability leads
     ↓
4. CLOSE — Better targeting = higher conversion rates and lower cost per deal
     ↓
(Back to SHARE — cycle repeats monthly)
```

Clients who actively participate in the Feedback Loop see significantly better results over time. **Getting clients to engage with the Feedback Loop is one of CS's most important jobs.**

### Key Product Concepts

**BuyBox:** Each client has a custom BuyBox — their specific targeting criteria for property acquisition. It defines which properties appear in their data lists. CSMs help clients refine their BuyBox through regular audits.

**Hidden Gems:** Properties that lack standard data (year built, last sale date). Most providers skip these. Our proprietary AVM (Automated Valuation Model) can value them, giving our clients access to properties their competition literally cannot see.

**Absentee Owner Levels:**
- Level 0: Owner lives at the property (lowest motivation)
- Level 1: Owner in-state, different address (moderate motivation)
- Level 2: Owner out-of-state (highest motivation — best targets)

**Non-Disclosure States (12):** Alaska, Idaho, Kansas, Louisiana, Mississippi, Missouri, Montana, New Mexico, North Dakota, Texas, Utah, Wyoming — sale prices aren't public, making our proprietary data even more valuable in these markets.

### What Our Clients Do for a Living

Our clients are professional **real estate investors**. Most are **wholesalers** — they find motivated sellers, put properties under contract at below-market prices, and assign those contracts to end buyers for an assignment fee. They never take ownership of the property.

**The wholesale process:**
1. **Find** — Use our data to identify motivated sellers (distress signals, tax delinquency, probate, vacant properties)
2. **Contact** — Reach owners via direct mail, cold calling, SMS, or digital ads (often using our ad management)
3. **Negotiate** — Secure a purchase contract at below-market price
4. **Assign** — Find a cash buyer willing to pay more than the contract price
5. **Close** — Collect the assignment fee (the spread between contract price and buyer price)

Other strategies our clients use:

| Strategy | Description |
|---|---|
| **Fix & Flip** | Buy, renovate, resell at market value. Higher profit, higher risk. |
| **Novation** | Market the property on the seller's behalf, take a fee at closing. |
| **Wholetail** | Buy, minimal cleanup, list on MLS. |
| **Buy & Hold** | Purchase and rent for recurring income. |
| **Creative Financing** | Seller financing, subject-to, lease options. Minimal cash required. |
| **Subject To** | Take over existing mortgage payments without formally assuming the loan. |

### Motivated Sellers — The Source of Deals

Our data helps clients find **motivated sellers** — property owners under pressure to sell. The industry calls these the **5 D's**:

| Signal | Description |
|---|---|
| **Death** | Estate/probate properties where heirs want to liquidate |
| **Divorce** | Court-ordered property disposition |
| **Delinquency** | Tax or mortgage delinquency creating urgency |
| **Distress** | Code violations, condemned properties, financial hardship |
| **Displacement/Downsizing** | Absentee owners, vacant properties, elderly owners with 25+ year ownership |

- **Lead-to-contract ratio** — Target: 1:35 (one deal per 35 leads)
- **Speed** — Am I finding deals faster than my competition?
- **Exclusivity value** — Am I competing against fewer people in my market?

**CS Mission:** Boost client ROI to 5x and achieve a 1:35 lead-to-contract ratio. Make success in real estate investing a guarantee, not just a goal.

## 5. Client Lifecycle — What CS Owns

### The Full Journey

```
[Marketing generates lead] → [Sales qualifies & closes]
    → HANDOFF TO CS (⚠️ Critical handoff #1)
    → Registration & Welcome
    → BuyBox Setup Call (⚠️ Critical handoff #2: gap >2 weeks = buyer's remorse)
    → First Fulfillment (~2 weeks)
    → Onboarding Call
    → Monthly Fulfillments + Engagement Calls (⚠️ Critical handoff #3: no proactive scheduling = silent churn)
    → Ongoing: Expansion / QBR / Renewal
    → At-Risk → Intervention → Retained or Churned
```

**The 3 Critical Handoff Points:**
1. **Sales → CS:** Incomplete notes = blind start, trust erosion with client
2. **BuyBox Call → First Fulfillment:** Gap longer than 2 weeks = excitement fade, buyer's remorse
3. **Post-Onboarding → Ongoing Engagement:** No proactive scheduling = client disengages silently

### Onboarding (Post-Close)

1. **Welcome email + CSM introduction** — Immediate after close
2. **BuyBox Setup Call** — Define targeting criteria (property types, price range, distress signals, geography)
3. **First Fulfillment** — Initial data delivery (~2 weeks after BuyBox call)
4. **Onboarding Call** — Walk through platform, tools, Feedback Loop setup, expectations
5. **First results review** — Check-in at week 2-4 to review lead flow and ad performance
6. **Graduation** — Onboarding complete, transition to ongoing monthly management

**Key metric:** Time to Value — days from signup to first meaningful outcome (first qualified lead, first deal under contract from our data).

### Ongoing Management (Monthly Cycle)

- **Monthly fulfillment delivery** — Fresh data lists based on their BuyBox
- **Engagement calls** — Regular CSM strategy calls (target: every 2-3 weeks per client)
- **BuyBox audits** — Quarterly review and optimization of targeting criteria
- **Feedback Loop participation** — Client reports results so AI can optimize
- **Ad performance reviews** — ROAS, spend, leads generated (if they have ad management)
- **Health monitoring** — Automated risk scoring + manual CSM review
- **Proactive outreach** — Don't wait for problems to surface

## 6. CS Metrics & KPIs

### Primary KPI

**MRR Retention Rate: 97% monthly target**

Formula: 1 - (Net Revenue Lost / Starting MRR)

Net Revenue Lost = Cancellations + Pauses/Holds - Won Back MRR

### Revenue Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **MRR** | Monthly Recurring Revenue from all active subscriptions | Core revenue health |
| **NRR (Net Revenue Retention)** | (Starting MRR + Expansion - Contraction - Churn) / Starting MRR | >100% = growing without new sales |
| **Gross Churn Rate** | MRR lost to cancellations / Starting MRR | Direct measure of client loss |
| **Net Churn Rate** | (Churn MRR - Expansion MRR) / Starting MRR | Churn offset by growth |
| **MRR at Risk** | MRR from clients flagged as at-risk | How much revenue is in danger |
| **MRR at Risk %** | At-risk MRR / Total active MRR | Green: <15%, Yellow: 15-20%, Red: >20% |
| **Expansion Revenue** | Additional MRR from upsells | Revenue growth from existing clients |
| **Contraction Revenue** | MRR lost from downgrades | Early warning of dissatisfaction |

## 9. Common CS Scenarios

### Client at risk of churning

**Signs:** Health score dropped, declining logins, more support tickets, ad performance down, payment issues, missing calls.

**Playbook:**
1. Review client profile and all metrics (health, engagement, billing, tickets, ad performance)
2. Identify root cause — is it performance, budget, market conditions, personal, or relationship?
3. Schedule intervention call within 48 hours
4. Prepare recovery plan (BuyBox refresh, ad optimization, training, county swap, pricing discussion)
5. Log everything in Salesmate
6. Monitor weekly until health score stabilizes above 3.5

### Client wants to cancel

**Retention playbook:**
1. Send approved retention email (immediately)
2. Call within 6 hours
3. SMS follow-up if no connection
4. On the call: understand the real reason, present data on their ROI, offer solutions
5. If fee negotiation needed: 75% → 50% → escalate to Head of CS if below 25%
6. If client churns: complete cancellation autopsy within 48 hours

### Client wants to expand

**Signs:** Asking about new counties, increasing ad budget, referring other investors, expressing satisfaction.

**Playbook:**
1. Check county seat availability
2. Prepare expansion proposal with projected ROI based on current performance
3. Coordinate pricing with sales if needed
4. Onboard new counties (BuyBox update, data setup, ad launch)
5. Track expansion revenue

### New client onboarding

**Playbook:**
1. Welcome email + CSM introduction within 24 hours of close
2. BuyBox Setup Call — define all targeting criteria
3. First fulfillment delivery (~2 weeks)
4. Onboarding call — platform walkthrough, Feedback Loop setup
5. Check-ins at week 1, 2, and 4
6. Graduate when milestones complete and first results achieved
7. Transition to monthly engagement cadence

### Preparing a QBR

**Playbook:**
1. Pull client metrics: MRR, ad performance, lead volume, deals closed, ROI
2. Review activity history: calls, emails, support tickets, Feedback Loop submissions
3. Audit BuyBox — is targeting still optimal?
4. Identify wins to highlight and areas for improvement
5. Prepare expansion recommendations if appropriate
6. Create presentation/summary with clear ROI narrative
7. Follow up with action items, deadlines, and next QBR date

### Monthly churn analysis

**Playbook:**
1. Pull churn data: gross churn rate, lost MRR, churned client count
2. Complete cancellation autopsy for each lost client
3. Categorize by reason: performance, budget, market, competitor, personal
4. Look for patterns: CSM-specific? County type? Tier? Tenure? Budget level?
5. Propose preventive measures
6. Update forecasts and NRR projections
7. Present findings in CS L10
